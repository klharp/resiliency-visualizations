VISUALIZING SUPPORTING DATA FOR PEARL RESILIENCE SCORE

This dashboard is intended as an overview of U.S. climate-related disasters and their impacts.

The visualizations are intended to help Pearl develop the Pearl Resilience Score (PRS) to help to identify a home's resilience against climate-related hazards.

These hazards have been identified as:

  • Extreme heat / drought
  • Extreme cold / winter storms
  • Storms (wind, hail, lightning)
  • Floods (inland, coastal)
  • Tornados
  • Hurricanes / Tropical storms
  • Wildfires
  • Earthquakes

Uses of the PRS will include the following:

  • A homeowner or homebuyer to make purchase or renovation decsions.
  • An insurance company to make underwriting decisions.
  • A public planning agency to allocate rebates and incentives for improvements and to inform land use and other public policy issues.

FEMA Declared Disasters

A disaster is declared by the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act: "All requests for a declaration by the President that a major disaster exists shall be made by the governor of the affected state or territory, or by the tribal leader." (FEMA)

  • The extent of the disaster.
  • Its impact on individuals and public facilities.
  • The types of federal assistance that may be needed.

Hazard Events & Costs

"Total Hazard Events (2000-2021)" displays the sum of annual hazard events. Note: the data for the wildfires includes all wildfire events in the US. An extreme event for a wildfire has not been defined.

"Total Hazard Costs (2000-2021)" displays the costs of annual hazard events.

"Top 25 Hazards" shows the top hazard events in terms of cost from 2000-2021.

"Hazard Events per State (2000-2021)" animates the annual hazard events with each state's housing price index (HPI). The HPI is a broad measure of the movement of single-family house prices. The HPI is a weighted, repeat-sales index, meaning that it measures average price changes in repeat sales or refinancings on the same properties.

Data

Data for the dashboard was derived from various sources:

  • National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI) 2021 Billion-dollar Disaster Report
  • National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) Storm Events Database
  • U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey
  • Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)
  • National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) Earthquake Data
  • National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NIFC) Wildfire Data
  • Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA) Housing Price Indes (HPI)
  • First Street Foundation Publicly Available Data (Flooding/Wildfires)
