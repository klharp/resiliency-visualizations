Wildfires

"Knowing wildfire risk can spell the difference between saving or losing a home, or even saving lives. Informed homeowners in fire country would be more likely to have evacuation plans. They would be more likely to take fire-proofing steps. Cutting back flammable brush and making a roof or eaves more fire-resistant greatly increases the odds that, after a wildfire, people will still have a home to which they can return." (NPR, 2020)

According to an analysis by Verisk, over 4.5 million U.S. homes are identified at high or at extreme risk of wildfire. In California alone, there were more than 2 million properties at high to extreme wildfire risk in 2021. (Verisk, 2021)

State name, county name, number of properties , and population are displayed when hovering over each county. Counties with the lightest color yellow have missing Fire Factor data.

The risk value (Fire Factor) was developed by First Street Foundation which is a non-profit dedicated to quantifying and communicating risks to inform Americans of their risks from environmental changes.

Fire Factor determine the risk of being impacted by wildfire by considering:

  • property's building materials
  • distance to fire risk areas
  • burnable materials, such as vegetation)
